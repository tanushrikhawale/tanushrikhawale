About Me

I'm passionate about web development and transforming ideas into digital experiences through code. Currently learning HTML, CSS and JavaScript at the Digital Fabrication Lab, SSGMCE.

Building the Web, One Tag at a Time

Day 1: Understanding the Foundation

Stepping into HTML felt like unlocking the core of the web. Every webpage has a well-defined structure and today, I explored how it all begins.

  • The Essential Skeleton: HTML pages are built using <html>, <head> and <body>—the backbone of any webpage.
  • Organizing Content: Headings (<h1> to <h6>) create a structured flow, making information clear and readable.
  • Making Pages Dynamic: Adding hyperlinks (<a>) and images (<img>) transformed a blank page into something meaningful.

Understanding these fundamentals showed me that a webpage is more than just text—it's a structured blueprint ready for design.

Day 2: Structuring Content with Purpose

Raw text alone isn't enough; structure and readability are just as important. Today was all about organizing content effectively.

  • Enhancing Readability: Formatting text using bold, italics and underline made key points stand out.
  • Understanding Layout Elements: <div> and <span> helped group content logically, making future styling easier.
  • The Power of Semantic HTML: Using <header>, <footer>, and <section> instead of generic divs improved structure, accessibility, and readability.

This day reinforced that well-structured HTML leads to a cleaner, more maintainable webpage.

Day 3: Making Content Interactive

Websites aren't just static—they organize and collect data. Today, I explored how HTML structures information.

  • Lists for Better Organization: Unordered (<ul>) and ordered (<ol>) lists make content easier to navigate.
  • Tables for Data Presentation: Structuring information using <table>, <th>, <tr>, and <td> made complex data more readable.
  • Forms for User Interaction: Input fields, checkboxes, and buttons introduced the first steps of user engagement.

Seeing how users can interact with a webpage was a turning point—it's not just about displaying content but also gathering input efficiently.

Looking Ahead

In just three days, I've gone from understanding basic HTML to creating structured and interactive content. But this is only the beginning.

Next, I'll explore CSS and JavaScript to bring design and functionality into my pages, making them truly engaging.

This journey at the Digital Fabrication Lab isn't just about learning a language—it's about shaping ideas into digital experiences.

More to come soon!
